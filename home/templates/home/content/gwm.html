Groundwater Management (GWM)

Groundwater management (GWM) ensures the sustainable use of water resources. It involves groundwater potential assessment, resource estimation, and managed aquifer recharge (MAR) systems.

Groundwater Potential Assessment

Applications:

  • Identifies optimal well locations using hydrogeological and geophysical methods.
  • Reduces over-extraction and ensures efficient groundwater utilization.
  • Uses remote sensing and GIS-based multi-criteria analysis to delineate high-potential zones.

Resource Estimation

Applications:

  • Determines groundwater availability using recharge-discharge balance models.
  • Assesses contamination risks from industrial, agricultural, and urban sources.
  • Identifies areas prone to over-extraction, saline intrusion, and contamination.

Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR)

Applications:

  • Assesses groundwater recharge rates at different spatial scales.
  • Models climate change impacts on aquifer recharge and baseflow.
  • Uses GIS-based suitability mapping for artificial recharge projects.

River-Aquifer Interaction

Applications:

  • Quantifies groundwater contribution to river flow using hydrograph separation and isotopic analysis.
  • Analyzes the impact of climate change on river baseflow.
  • Develops strategies such as controlled groundwater pumping to maintain ecological balance.

Why This Matters?

Ensuring sustainable water resource management is crucial for urban and rural landscapes. These methods support climate resilience, disaster mitigation, and provide insights for policymakers.
