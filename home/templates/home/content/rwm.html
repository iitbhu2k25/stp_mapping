River Water Management (RWM)

Efficient River Water Management (RWM) is essential for sustainable water resource utilization, flood control, pollution management, and ecosystem preservation. This module provides advanced tools for river system assessment, forecasting, and decision-making.

Resource Estimation

Understanding river water availability, storage, and quality is fundamental for sustainable planning. This module integrates hydrological modeling, remote sensing, and real-time monitoring for comprehensive resource estimation.

  • Water Availability: Estimates river discharge and seasonal variations for water allocation planning.
  • Volumetric Flux & Storage: Quantifies river flow rates using GIS and remote sensing.
  • Water Quality Assessment: Monitors physical, chemical, and biological parameters.
  • Vulnerable Reaches: Identifies pollution hotspots and sensitive ecological zones.
  • Short-Term Contamination: Predicts contamination spikes during festivals and cultural activities.

Flood Forecasting & Management

Flooding poses severe risks to life, infrastructure, and ecosystems. This module provides real-time flood forecasting and risk assessment tools to enhance preparedness and mitigation efforts.

  • Flood Simulations: Uses hydrodynamic models to predict flood scenarios.
  • River Routing: Models floodwater movement and optimizes dam releases.
  • Contaminant Transport: Tracks pollutants to support water quality policies.

Water Bodies Management

  • Storage Estimation: Monitors reservoir variations in real-time.
  • Reservoir Operations: Plans water releases based on climate and agriculture needs.
  • Water Quality Monitoring: Tracks dissolved oxygen, turbidity, and pollution sources.

Wastewater Treatment

  • Pollution Inventory: Maps pollution sources along river stretches.
  • STP Site Selection: Uses GIS to identify optimal wastewater treatment locations.
  • Technology Recommendations: Proposes effective wastewater treatment methods.
